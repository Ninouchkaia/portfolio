Macrophages in the tumor microenvironment can adopt pro- or anti-tumoral phenotypes depending on their molecular environment.  
In chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL), a subset of macrophages, termed **nurse-like cells (NLCs)**, exhibit tumor-supportive properties that promote leukemic cell survival and immune escape.

This project investigated the regulatory mechanisms driving macrophage polarization in CLL through transcriptomic and network-based modeling approaches.
